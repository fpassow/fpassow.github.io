Bootstrap for Engineers

I once sang the praises of "Cro-Magnon HTML", ancient, unstyled html which loaded quickly, communicated clearly, displayed correctly on every imaginable device, and never broke. It was functional. It did the job. I might have even used the word "cosmetic" in a disparaging way.

But to the the modern eye, unstyled HTML does look like poo-with-serifs. It isn't a nice thing to do to inocent readers. And, let's admit it, we really do like it when our pages look better.

Easy Wins

There are plenty of bootstrap tutorials out there. And they are great for learning Bootstrap end-to-end. On this page, I will concentrate on easy ways to apply it to an existing page to make the most difference with the least effort.

First, here's a template for an HTML5 page that pulls bootstrap from a public Content Delivery Network, and adds one META tag and one DIV to make it all work.

This gives a number of improvements, including nicer fonts, automatic styling of <pre> and <blockquote> tags, and nice styling if you use a <footer> tag in your blockquote.


<html lang="en">
  <head>
    <meta charset="utf-8">
    <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1">
    <link rel="stylesheet" href="https://maxcdn.bootstrapcdn.com/bootstrap/3.3.7/css/bootstrap.min.css">
  </head>
  <body>
    <div class="container">
    
    
    </div>
  </body>
</html>
